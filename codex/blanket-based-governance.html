Blanket-Based Governance

A collective coping mechanism where horizontal existence and soft boundaries become the primary governance model for emotional recovery.

AI Summary
Blanket-Based Governance is an emotional survival strategy used by households during periods of extreme social exhaustion or burnout. It prioritizes "horizontal functionality," minimal verbal interaction, and the deployment of "Burrito Mode" as a cultural norm.

Definition

This doctrine emerges when the collective social battery of a household drops below 5%. In Blanket-Based Governance, the standard rules of productivity are suspended in favor of soft, cushioned environments and temperature control.

Emotional Meaning

It symbolizes a retreat to the "embryonic state." The household is collectively acknowledging that the external world is currently too "loud" or "bright." By moving to horizontal surfaces, members reduce the gravity of their emotional burdens.
